IEEE SB STM - Our Journey

The IEEE Student Branch at St. Thomas College of Engineering and Technology, Kannur (IEEE SB STM), established in December 2023, is a vibrant student-led community driven by innovation, learning, and impact. Guided by our Branch Counsellor, Dr. Sreekesh Namboodiri T, the branch creates opportunities for students to explore technology beyond classrooms.

Our Mission & Values

IEEE SB STM empowers students through hands-on workshops, tech talks, hackathons, competitions, and collaborative projects that focus on real-world problem solving. We encourage curiosity, creativity, and collaboration while helping members build technical expertise, leadership skills, and a strong professional network.

Rooted in IEEE's core values of integrity, inclusiveness, global outlook, and service to humanity, IEEE SB STM strives to nurture future-ready engineers who innovate responsibly and contribute meaningfully to society and the global engineering community.

IEEE Societies and Affinity Groups

IEEE societies and affinity groups, such as the Computer Society (CS), Power & Energy Society (PES), and Women in Engineering (WIE), are specialized communities within the IEEE that provide technical, professional, and networking opportunities for members. These groups operate at the section, chapter, or student branch level, focusing on specific technologies or professional development goals.

IEEE Societies

IEEE Computer Society (CS)

Known as the leading organization for computing professionals, with over 85,000 members. It focuses on computer science, hardware, software, and networking, providing resources like publications, journals, and conferences.

IEEE Power & Energy Society (PES)

Focuses on the science and engineering of electric power and energy. It facilitates technical knowledge sharing, standards development, and education in the power industry.Societies like the Power Electronics Society (PELS) and Industrial Applications Society (IAS) often collaborate with PES.

Detailed Event Reports

Workshop on Flutter

Date: 11 November 2023

Time: 09:30 AM - 11:35 AM

Venue: Programming Lab 1, STM

Participants: 31

Flutter workshop session organised by IEEE Student Branch of St. Thomas College of Engineering and Technology Kannur was an enlightening experience for those seeking to enhance their knowledge about Flutter, an open-source UI software development toolkit created by Google. The workshop was handled by guest speakers Mr. Arjun P J, Mr. Vaishakh, and Ms. Aryasree from Government College of Engineering Kannur.

The session provided hands-on experience in building cross-platform applications, enabling participants to gain practical knowledge on Flutter. The interactive session included presentations and practical demonstrations. Participants provided positive feedback on the workshop content, marking it as a successful endeavor by IEEE SB STM to educate students in the latest development tools.

Execom Selection

Date: 20 November 2023

Time: 10:30 AM - 11:30 AM

Venue: Library, STM

Participants: 31

The execom members selection event was a significant initiative aimed at appointing new members to the executive committee and fostering leadership within the IEEE community. The event served as a platform for students to engage actively in organizational roles and contribute to the growth of IEEE at St. Thomas College.

The selection featured a rigorous process designed to identify individuals with passion for IEEE activities and leadership qualities. Positions assigned included Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Technical Coordinator, Link Rep, and more. The event facilitated networking among IEEE members and provided opportunities for personal and professional development.

Inauguration of IEEE Student Branch

Date: 7 December 2023

Time: 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM

The ceremony featured a symbolic lamp lighting ceremony and presentation of mementos.

Trivia - Technical Quiz Competition

Date: 23 February 2024

Time: Two-Round Format

Venue: Round 1: Online | Round 2: Seminar Hall 1

Participants: 61

An exciting two-round technical quiz competition where participants showcased their expertise across diverse technical concepts. The first round was conducted online with 20 questions for 20 marks. Participants scoring above 18 marks were selected for the second round held in Seminar Hall 1.

The quiz covered topics including computer science, engineering principles, emerging technologies, and innovations. Participants demonstrated quick thinking, problem-solving abilities, and comprehensive understanding. The event fostered a spirit of healthy competition and collaborative learning within the IEEE community.

Workshop on Design and Commissioning of Solar Power Plants (Part 1)

Date: 15 March 2024

Time: 09:00 AM - 10:30 AM

Venue: Seminar Hall 1

Participants: 61

Sponsor: Sonic Power Solutions

The workshop on solar power plants was led by Dr. Shinu Mathew John (Principal, STM). The program included an introduction to solar energy solutions, covering both on-grid and off-grid solar power plants. Dr. Shinu Mathew John shared personal experiences with real-life examples, explaining key electrical concepts such as voltage and current.

The workshop provided a comprehensive introduction to solar energy, covering design aspects, commissioning processes, and emphasized the importance of collaboration and knowledge sharing for successful project implementation. Participants gained valuable learning experience setting the stage for advanced sessions.

Workshop on Design and Commissioning of Solar Power Plants (Part 2)

Date: 15 April 2024

Time: 03:00 PM - 04:30 PM

Venue: Seminar Hall 1

Participants: 47

Partners: IEEE SB STM, NSS Unit 310, Nature Club STM | Sponsor: Sonic Power Solutions

Part 2 of the solar power workshop was a hands-on session led by Dr. Shinu Mathew John. The session covered the complete process of creating a solar power plant, from initial site assessment to ongoing maintenance. Key topics included site selection, detailed planning, panel orientation, energy storage batteries, MPPT controller and inverter connections.

Participants learned about testing procedures, grid connection processes, and maintenance strategies essential for long-term plant efficiency. The workshop emphasized meticulous planning and careful installation procedures to ensure sustainable energy production.
